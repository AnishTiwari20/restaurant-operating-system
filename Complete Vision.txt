# Product Requirements Document (PRD)

## Project Name

RestaurantOS

## Vision

I want to build a cloud-based SaaS platform for restaurants. The goal is to create one application that can be used by thousands of restaurants instead of developing custom software for each one.

Every restaurant will have its own account, menu, orders, customers, tables, analytics, and settings, while all restaurants use the same underlying platform (multi-tenant architecture).

The software should be modern, responsive, secure, and easy enough for a restaurant owner with minimal technical knowledge to use.

---

# Users

There are five types of users.

### 1. Customer

A customer visits the restaurant, sits at a table, scans the QR code, views the menu, places an order, pays (or selects pay later if enabled), tracks order status, and gives feedback.

---

### 2. Restaurant Owner

The owner manages the restaurant.

The owner can:

* Edit menu
* Add categories
* Upload food images
* Change prices
* Mark dishes unavailable
* View live orders
* Manage tables
* Manage staff
* View analytics
* Configure taxes
* Configure payment methods
* View reports
* Manage coupons and offers

---

### 3. Kitchen Staff

Kitchen staff only see incoming orders.

They should not see financial information.

They can update order status:

Received

↓

Preparing

↓

Ready

---

### 4. Waiter

Waiters can:

* View tables
* Check order status
* Mark food served
* Place manual orders if needed
* Collect cash payments if enabled

---

### 5. Super Admin (Platform Owner)

This is me.

I manage every restaurant on the platform.

I can:

* Create restaurant accounts
* Suspend restaurants
* View subscription status
* Manage pricing plans
* View platform analytics
* Manage support tickets
* Send announcements

---

# Customer Flow

1. Customer enters restaurant.
2. Customer sits at Table 5.
3. Customer scans QR code.
4. Menu opens in browser (no app installation).
5. Customer browses menu.
6. Customer adds items to cart.
7. Customer customizes food (extra cheese, less spicy, etc.).
8. Customer checks out.
9. Payment is completed or payment method selected.
10. Order immediately appears in restaurant dashboard.
11. Kitchen receives the order.
12. Kitchen updates status.
13. Waiter serves food.
14. Customer leaves feedback.

# Goal

The objective is to build software that becomes the operating system for independent restaurants. QR ordering is only the starting point. Over time, the platform should evolve into a complete restaurant management ecosystem covering ordering, payments, kitchen operations, analytics, customer engagement, and business insights, while remaining simple enough for small and medium-sized restaurants to adopt.
